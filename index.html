Welcome

Welcome to my academic homepage. I'm currently an East Asian Core Observatories Association (EACOA) fellow at the National Astronomical Observatory of Japan (NAOJ). My work focuses on supermassive black hole accretion and feedback, with a particular interest in the multi-phase interstellar medium in nearby galaxies. Multi-wavelength observations targeting on these nearest central engines hold the key to unravel the mysterious nature of the feeding and feedback of the SMBH, and thus open a door for our understanding of galaxy evolution. M31 is my favorite galaxy. It is the closest massive spiral, and is regarded as the sister galaxy to our Milky Way, yet there are many fascinating puzzles burried in its beautiful spirals, awaiting us to explore.
