Research

Connecting gas, stars, and black holes across galactic nuclei.

I combine radio, submillimeter, infrared, optical, and X-ray observations to build a spatially resolved view of nearby galaxies.

01

Galactic nuclei

Using M31, M81, and other nearby low-luminosity active galactic nuclei as laboratories for black hole feeding, feedback, and nuclear activity.

  • M31
  • M81
  • LINERs

02

Multiphase gas

Tracing molecular, atomic, ionized, and hot gas to understand the physical state, energy balance, and kinematics of the interstellar medium.

  • CO
  • H I
  • Ionized gas
  • Dust

03

SMBH–galaxy co-evolution

Studying how accretion and energetic feedback interact with nuclear star clusters, circumnuclear structures, and galaxy-scale environments.

  • Accretion
  • Feedback
  • Outflows

04

Star formation

Measuring molecular cloud properties and the connection between gas supply, star formation efficiency, and quenching in nearby disks.

  • Clouds
  • Quenching
  • Disk galaxies
