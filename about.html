About

Astronomy, curiosity, and a close look at our galactic neighbors.

I am an EACOA Fellow at KASI in Daejeon, South Korea. My path—from Sichuan to Nanjing, Beijing, Tokyo, and Daejeon—has been driven by a long-standing curiosity about how the universe works.

About Me

I was born in Sichuan Province, China, and I am the only person in my family to have attended college. From an early age, I was fascinated by stories about scientists such as Albert Einstein and Marie Curie. When I was four years old, I raised my hand in class and announced that I wanted to become a physicist.

Years later, I entered university and chose to study astronomy and astrophysics, bringing me close to that childhood dream. I am happy to find that I am still the same girl who loves looking at the night sky and thinking about the big questions of the universe.

Outside astronomy, I enjoy jogging, hiking, swimming, and other activities that bring me closer to nature. Surfing is still on my list of things to try. I also enjoy reading across many subjects, love animals—especially whales—and listen to rock music; Radiohead is my favorite band.

I am also a big fan of Japanese animation. Haikyu!! is my favorite series, followed by Fullmetal Alchemist. I am always open to new experiences and possibilities, and I hope to continue exploring more of the world in the future.

I am currently an East Asian Core Observatories Association (EACOA) Fellow at the Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute (KASI), following my EACOA appointment at the National Astronomical Observatory of Japan (NAOJ). My research focuses on star formation, the interstellar medium, and multi-wavelength observations of nearby galaxies, with a particular interest in black hole accretion and active galactic nucleus feedback.
